We present a parameter-free causal-wave transport law with five
coefficients $(\alpha_{\xi}, D_{\Omega}, \beta_{\pi}, \gamma,
\varepsilon_{\rm sync}^{2})$ derived from a bounded-operator readout
of a finite relational lattice, and audit ten cross-sector benchmark
landings against external anchors (PDG, Planck, NuFIT, BBN). All
ten landings close to EXACT-tier ($|r|\le 0.01\%$) or PRECISE-tier
($|r|\le 2.5\%$) against the registered anchors, with no fitted
parameter at the closure level. Five $\mathcal R$-rational
conditions $(C_{1},\ldots,C_{5})$ further reduce the five
coefficients to integer combinations of the two structural inputs
$(d, N_{\rm gen})\!=\!(4,3)$, giving the closed System-$\mathcal R$
tuple $(9/10, 67/80, 15/16, 1/10, 1/20)$ with residuals $\le 0.27\%$
against the lattice-extracted coefficients. The Bekenstein--Hawking
identification $S/A\!=\!1/4$ closes EXACT under $\mathcal R$ via
$\alpha_{\xi}/2 - 2\gamma = 1/4$, with the unique integer solution
$N\!=\!3$ for the inverse-dimension prefactor. A four-projector
spectral-uniqueness theorem (Reed--Simon VIII.5) establishes that
the bounded-operator readout admits the four-channel decomposition
$(\Pi_{\xi}, \Pi_{\Omega}, \Pi_{\rm common}, \Pi_{\rm sync})$
\emph{up to thresholds} and identifies the fluctuation-dissipation
identity $C_{3}: \varepsilon_{\rm sync}^{2}\!=\!\gamma/2$. A
look-elsewhere null on 5000 small-rational alternative-target
5-tuples returns 0/5000 simultaneous matches at residual $\le 0.30\%$
($p\!<\!2\!\times\!10^{-4}$), isolating the canonical assignment as
the unique self-consistent fixed point. Three frozen prospective
predictions are registered with structural-form locks:
$\delta_{\rm CP}$ (NuFIT), $\Omega_{\rm DM} h^{2}$ (DESI Year-3),
$H_{0}$ (LiteBIRD/CMB-S4). See P0~\cite{Paper0} for corpus-wide
claim grammar and falsifiers.
